The A-team, running presented to you by Alastair Leslie.

Welcome. The Coach Alistair has endurance sport in his blood, it is like a disease. After years of competing at the highest level as both a runner and a triathlete what else would you call it? Alistair started coaching informally in 2003, but it took him 10yrs (and a well timed redundancy) to make it formal, and for A-team to be born in October 2013. Alistair enjoys nothing more than seeing his athletes realise they can push the boundaries of what they thought was possible. Achieve what seemed impossible, and then look forward to the next challenge. You could be coming off the couch to run for 5min without a walk, or it could be you want to finish a 50km bike event; or maybe you want to improve your Marathon or Ironman PB. It doesn’t matter where you are starting from Alistair can help you achieve your goals. Alistair not only has years of experience, he also has a Bachelors Degree in Sport Science, and is an accredited and registered with Triathlon and Athletics New Zealand. Who is this Alastair Leslie

Alastair Leslie is a well-respected endurance and running coach based in Wellington, New Zealand, known for his commitment to developing athletes across all levels of performance. His journey in sport began at a young age, where he developed a strong background in running and endurance training, which later evolved into a passion for coaching and athlete development. Over the years, Alastair has built a solid coaching reputation through his work with a wide range of athletes, from beginners to competitive runners. His achievements are reflected in the progression of the athletes he coaches, many of whom have improved personal bests, successfully completed endurance events, and performed strongly in regional and national-level competitions. His ability to consistently guide athletes toward measurable improvement highlights his effectiveness as a coach. Alastair brings a practical and experience-driven approach to coaching, focusing on consistency, structured training, and long-term athlete development. He is known for designing programmes that not only enhance performance but are also sustainable alongside school, work, and everyday life. This balance has been a key factor in helping athletes maintain motivation and achieve long-term success. As a coach, Alastair emphasises an athlete-centred philosophy, tailoring training plans to suit individual goals—whether that is improving race times, building endurance, or preparing for competition. He has contributed to the development of athletes competing in cross country, road racing, and other endurance events, supporting them to perform at their best. Through his coaching in Wellington, Alastair continues to make a positive impact within the running community. His philosophy centres on progression, resilience, and helping athletes realise their full potential through commitment and hard work.
